TRUCKERS RULES

Attention Trucking Community

APPOINTMENTS ARE REQUIRED FOR ALL GATE MOVES. PLEASE GO TO EMODAL TO MAKE APPOINTMENT

All demurrage fees for Evergreen, CMA and COSCO will be collected via eModal.  OOCL and APL will continue to collect their own demurrage fees.

When entering the terminal truckers will be required to press a button to receive a gate pass that contains a gate pass number and bar code.  When pressing the button this will also activate the gate arm to allow truckers onto the terminal.  When truckers arrive at the in-gate lane pedestals they will need to insert their gate pass into the kiosk to have the bar code read.  This will initiate processing into the terminal.

Safety and Terminal Rules:

1.) Speed limit is 10 mph throughout the terminal and 5 mph through roadability.

2.) No cell phone use while driving.

3.) Obey all posted stop signs and stop bars.

4.) Stop before entering roadability and do not leave prior to being cleared.

5.) Wear proper P.P.E. (hi-visibility vests, gloves, and closed toed shoes).

6.) Containers need to be locked down at roadability. 

7.) All trucks are required to proceed to the queuing area when it is open. Queuing area opens 2 hours prior to the gate opening.

8.) No photography or videotaping inside the terminal.

9.) Gaterunners will be charged a fee of $197.00. 

T.W.I.C Cards:

No truck drivers will be allowed into Pierce County Terminal without a valid Transportation Workers Identification Credential.  For further information please refer to: https://www.tsa.gov/for-industry/twic

CHASSIS USAGE:

Evergreen containers use Evergreen or trucker-owned chassis only. COSCO, OOCL, APL and CMA containers use TRAC or trucker-owned chassis only.

Roadability Rules:

All chassis, except for those chassis owned by an independent truck driver or owned by an independent non PMA affiliated trucking company and identified and proven as such, shall go through roadability.

If a driver with a long term leased chassis chooses to go through roadability and fails any part of the 8 part inspections, they may have mechanics make the necessary repairs or the container shall be removed and the driver may exit the facility.

Truck Driver Training Request:

All training request should be sent to ABPI Customer Service at customerservice-tiw@abpiusa.com.

DO NOT USE THE TOOLS AT ROADABILITY

All Hazardous and PTT documents should be sent to ABPI customer service at customerservice-tiw@abpiusa.com
